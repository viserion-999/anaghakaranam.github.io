Solving the problem of Lead Generation and the concept of Lean Analytics¶

What is the fuss all about?!¶

In this post we will cover the basics of two concepts – lean analytics & lead generation.¶

_Lean Analytics_

Inspired by a blog post about a book I was reading, I came across the concept of Lean Analytics. The Lean Analytics Cycle, aims to help you create a sustainable way to pick metrics that matter by tying them to fundamental business problems, creating hypotheses you can test and driving change in the business from the learnings you identify. The concept is similar to Lean Startup, we continuously improve, iterate and check upon results, and decide what to do.
The Lean Analytics Cycle is a simple four step iterative process.

- Step1: Figure out what to improve.¶

- Step2: Form the hypothesis.¶

- Step3:Create the experiment¶

- Step4: Measure and decide what to do¶

Step 2,3,4 are repeated continuously until we get the desired outcome. I will explain each step in detail as we dive into an experiment with lead generation.

_Lead Generation_

According to hubspot,

Lead generation is the process of attracting and converting strangers and prospects into someone who has indicated interest in your company's product or service. Some examples of lead generators are job applications, blog posts, coupons, live events, and online content.¶

"In simpler words, a good lead generation system in your organization will save you from being blocked/tagged as spam by customers!"¶

Although people will click on your website’s link, see your product at a grocery store what is the possibility of making them buy it?

Lead Generation is that small window, that tiny gap using which you can make the customer pick your product at the store.¶

You attract strangers as visitors, you generate leads and successfully convert them into your customers, who(fingers crossed) will eventually become your promoters!

Lets get to work already!¶

For our experiment, we have a beautiful website by Kayla dedicated to women’s healthy lifestyle, food and fitness.(Awareness) The blogger has lots of followers and she promises to email more tips, diet plans, recipes and exercises when people opt-in to do so using a subscribe button. (Lead Generation)

After a few emails of sending strategies, Kayla decides to host an online exercise session for all women and emails the same. (Prospect). After a few free online sessions, she hosts a package of 12 sessions with a discount offer to all her prospects (Sale)!

Step1: Figure out what to improve¶

The most crucial step is to decide what to improve, which is directly tied to the company’s KPIs and KRIs. Some examples can be to improve the number of visitors for a website, lower churn rate, increase conversion rate, getting more people to your restaurant etc. That one metric which will improve your KPIs, is critical for your business and will drive your profits for the next few months.

For our Kayla, it is to increase the number of people who visit her website and opt-in for her emails so that they can be the future customers to her online sessions.

Step2: Form a hypothesis¶

This step is all about collecting the data(exploratory data analysis), understanding the existing data, understanding customer behaviour – what are they buying, from where do customers come, what type of products are they combining and buying, what are loyal customers buying etc. In case we don’t have the data, this step is also about collecting the data.

For our data set, lead_gen.csv we have information on number of visitors from different channels and if they subscribed to the emails.

Step4: Measure and decide what to do¶

  • What is influencing user behavior? From the insights above, it is paged viewed. So maybe if we can get more pages viewed, we can increase number of subscribers? How would we do that? Once we do that we should run our algorithms again (go back to Step 2 and repeat from there)
  • Also, which country is not responding to ads? Which country is? Where can we invest more on ad campagins, where should we stop?

This is what step4 is all about. Measuring what we did, and going back to step2 and repeating the process till we get the desired results.
